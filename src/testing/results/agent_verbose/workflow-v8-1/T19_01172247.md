#### Method #109: Contraposition Inversion
**Definition:** Instead of what leads to success answer what guarantees failure then check if current solution does any of those. Known guarantees: async+consensus+failures=FLP violation; SP+IR+EFF+BB=M-S violation; universal termination proof=Halting violation.

**Application to VERITAS:**

**What guarantees failure for a mechanism design claiming all five properties?**

1. **Myerson-Satterthwaite Impossibility (1983):** No mechanism for bilateral trade with incomplete information can simultaneously achieve:
   - Strategyproofness (truthful bidding is dominant)
   - Individual Rationality (non-negative utility for participation)
   - Allocative Efficiency (maximizes total surplus)
   - Weak Budget Balance (no external subsidy)

2. **Green-Laffont Impossibility:** VCG mechanisms are the ONLY strategyproof and efficient mechanisms, but VCG mechanisms are NOT budget balanced except in very special cases.

**Does VERITAS do any of these failure-guaranteeing things?**

**Analysis of VERITAS claims:**
- Section 1: Claims "strategyproofness, individual rationality, efficiency, fairness, and budget balance"
- Section 4.1: Claims VCG payment scheme
- Section 4.2: Claims "exact budget balance" via redistribution
- Section 4.3: Claims "Individual Rationality Guarantee"
- Section 5.1: Claims "Truthful bidding is a dominant strategy"
- Section 5.2: Claims "allocative efficiency"

**CRITICAL FINDING DETECTED:**

The artifact claims to achieve ALL of:
1. Strategyproofness (dominant strategy truthfulness) - Section 5.1
2. Individual Rationality - Section 4.3
3. Allocative Efficiency - Section 5.2
4. Budget Balance - Section 4.2 ("exact budget balance")

This is a **direct violation of the Myerson-Satterthwaite Impossibility Theorem**.

The artifact's attempted solution (redistribution mechanism in 4.2) cannot resolve this:
- If redistribution maintains strategyproofness, it cannot achieve budget balance
- If redistribution achieves budget balance, it destroys strategyproofness
- The "per-agent redistribution" formula in 4.2 affects agent utilities in a way that depends on their actions, breaking the independence property required for strategyproofness

**Evidence from Artifact:**
- Section 4.2 states: "Redistribution(agent_i) = R x (contribution_i / sum_j contribution_j)"
- "contribution_i measures agent i's participation without winning"
- This means an agent's redistribution depends on their "contribution" which is influenced by their bidding behavior
- This breaks the core VCG property that payment is independent of own bid
