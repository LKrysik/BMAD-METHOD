### M6: Critical Challenge (Red-Flagged Claims Only)

#### C1: "exponential speedup over classical brute-force"

**Challenge:** Comparing to brute-force (O(2^n)) is trivial - classical heuristics (simulated annealing, genetic algorithms) also beat brute-force exponentially. True quantum advantage requires beating the BEST known classical algorithm.

**Verdict:** DEFEATED
**Correction:** Compare to simulated annealing or other state-of-art classical optimizers.

---

#### C2: "O(poly(n))" for Quantum Annealing

**Challenge:** Quantum annealing has no proven polynomial-time guarantee. The adiabatic theorem requires time T ∝ 1/Δ²min where Δmin (spectral gap) can be exponentially small for many optimization problems. This is an open research problem, not established fact.

**Verdict:** DEFEATED
**Correction:** State as "conjectured" or "heuristically observed", not as proven complexity bound.

---

#### C3: "99.7% global optimum"

**Challenge:** What benchmark? What problem instances? What problem sizes? Random QUBO? Structured QUBO? Without methodology, this number is unverifiable and possibly cherry-picked.

**Verdict:** WEAKENED
**Correction:** Provide benchmark suite, problem distribution, measurement methodology.

---

#### C4: "47ms average"

**Challenge:** Average over what? What hardware? What QPU? What problem size? What classical overhead? Without methodology, this number is meaningless.

**Verdict:** WEAKENED
**Correction:** Provide complete experimental setup and methodology.

---

#### C5: "speedup of 10^40"

**Challenge:** This assumes:
1. Quantum annealing is O(poly(n)) - UNPROVEN
2. Comparison is to brute force - WRONG BASELINE
3. n=150 is representative - CHERRY-PICKED

The 10^40 number is computed from false premises.

**Verdict:** DEFEATED
**Correction:** Remove or qualify heavily with assumptions.

---

#### C7: "Adiabatic theorem guarantees P > 0.99"

**Challenge:** The adiabatic theorem DOES guarantee ground state IF:
1. Evolution is slow enough (T >> 1/Δ²min)
2. Spectral gap Δmin is not exponentially small

For method selection QUBO, is the gap polynomial? This is not analyzed. The "guarantee" assumes what needs to be proven.

**Verdict:** WEAKENED
**Correction:** Analyze spectral gap for method selection QUBO, or acknowledge as assumption.

## Critical Findings

### F1: O(poly(n)) Complexity Claim is Unsubstantiated

**Evidence:** §6.1 states "Quantum Annealing | O(poly(n))" as fact.

**Problem:** Quantum annealing has no proven polynomial-time guarantee. Per established theory, annealing time scales as T ∝ 1/Δ²min where spectral gap Δmin can be exponentially small.

**Recommended action:** Remove claim or restate as "conjectured" / "heuristically observed".

---

### F2: "Quantum Advantage" Term Misused

**Evidence:** §6.3 claims "speedup of approximately 10^40 over exhaustive search."

**Problem:** Comparison to brute force (exhaustive search) is not quantum advantage. Quantum advantage means proven speedup over BEST KNOWN classical algorithm. Classical heuristics (simulated annealing) also beat brute force.

**Recommended action:** Compare to state-of-art classical optimizers or remove "quantum advantage" terminology.

---

### F3: 10^40 Speedup Claim Based on False Premises

**Evidence:** §6.3 calculates "S = T_classical / T_quantum = O(2^n) / O(poly(n))."

**Problem:**
1. T_quantum = O(poly(n)) is unproven (see F1)
2. T_classical = O(2^n) is brute force, not best classical (see F2)

**Recommended action:** Remove specific speedup number or provide rigorous derivation with explicit assumptions.
