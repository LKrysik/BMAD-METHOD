[F1] CRITICAL — Definitional Contradiction Between Claimed Perfect Forward Secrecy and Implemented Key Escrow
     Quote: The document claims "perfect forward secrecy" (Section 1) but the design specifies escrowing the session key for recovery: `encrypted_session_key: RSA-OAEP(RA_PubKey, session_key)` (Section 4.2).
     Location: Section 1, Section 2.1 (Layer 4), Section 4.2, Section 5.1, Section 6.1
     Pattern: PFS_ESCROW
     Comment: This is a fundamental flaw. Perfect Forward Secrecy (PFS) guarantees that the compromise of long-term keys does not compromise past session keys. Key escrow, by design, allows a long-term key (the Recovery Authority key) to decrypt past session keys, directly violating the core principle of PFS. The statement that recovery is possible "without compromising the forward secrecy properties" is false.
